# Acceleration and Center Fraction: Explanation + Metrics Analysis

## What is acceleration (`R`)?

In MRI reconstruction, **acceleration** means how aggressively k-space is undersampled.

- `R=4` means roughly one quarter of phase-encoding lines are sampled.
- `R=8` means roughly one eighth are sampled.

Higher `R` gives faster acquisition, but the inverse problem becomes harder and image quality usually drops unless reconstruction is strong.

---

## What is `center_fraction`?

`center_fraction` is the fraction of low-frequency k-space lines kept fully sampled in the center of k-space.

- Lower frequencies (k-space center) carry global contrast and structure.
- Higher frequencies (k-space periphery) carry fine details.

For the rows in `results/metrics_full.csv`, the configuration is:

- `R=4` with `center_fraction=0.08`
- `R=8` with `center_fraction=0.04`

So at higher acceleration, fewer central lines are retained, which further increases reconstruction difficulty.

## Quantitative analysis from `results/metrics_full.csv`

### Overall (`acquisition=ALL`)

| method | R | center_fraction | NMSE | PSNR | SSIM |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| zero_filled | 4 | 0.08 | 0.048356 | 29.3929 | 0.7962 |
| zero_filled | 8 | 0.04 | 0.108066 | 25.8668 | 0.6842 |
| unet | 4 | 0.08 | 0.011267 | 35.7541 | 0.9328 |
| unet | 8 | 0.04 | 0.023505 | 32.6063 | 0.9069 |

### Effect of increasing acceleration (4 -> 8)

- **zero_filled**
  - NMSE worsens by `2.235x` (0.04836 -> 0.10807)
  - PSNR drops by `3.53 dB`
  - SSIM drops by `0.1120`
- **unet**
  - NMSE worsens by `2.086x` (0.01127 -> 0.02351)
  - PSNR drops by `3.15 dB`
  - SSIM drops by `0.0259`

Interpretation: both degrade at `R=8`, but U-Net is much more robust (especially in SSIM).

### U-Net vs zero-filled (same acceleration)

- At `R=4`:
  - NMSE improves by `76.70%`
  - PSNR improves by `+6.36 dB`
  - SSIM improves by `+0.1366`
- At `R=8`:
  - NMSE improves by `78.25%`
  - PSNR improves by `+6.74 dB`
  - SSIM improves by `+0.2227`

Interpretation: learned reconstruction provides a large and consistent gain over zero-filled, and the relative gain is even larger at higher acceleration.

### Acquisition-level behavior

From `R=4` to `R=8`, U-Net NMSE rises by approximately:

- `1.982x` (AXT1)
- `2.238x` (AXT1POST)
- `2.101x` (AXT2)
- `1.727x` (AXFLAIR)

And zero-filled NMSE rises by approximately:

- `2.229x` (AXT1)
- `2.550x` (AXT1POST)
- `2.169x` (AXT2)
- `2.003x` (AXFLAIR)

Interpretation: all acquisitions become harder at higher acceleration, but U-Net degradation is milder than zero-filled across the board.

---

## Practical takeaway

- If scan time allows, `R=4` + larger center fraction is clearly better quality.
- If `R=8` is required for speed, U-Net reconstruction is strongly preferred over zero-filled.
- The `center_fraction` choice is part of this tradeoff: reducing it (0.08 -> 0.04) removes central k-space support and tends to hurt fidelity metrics.
